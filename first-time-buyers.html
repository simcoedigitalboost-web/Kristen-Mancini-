Programs & Incentives

Programs I'll help you access

These are real programs with real money—and most buyers don't even know they exist.

First-Time Home Buyer Incentive

5-10% toward your down payment

The federal government will contribute 5-10% of your home's purchase price toward your down payment through a shared equity program. You pay it back when you sell—but it can make the difference between buying now or waiting years.

Home Buyers' Plan (HBP)

Up to $60,000 from your RRSP

Withdraw up to $60,000 from your RRSP tax-free to use as a down payment. Buying with a partner? That's up to $120,000 combined. You have 15 years to repay it to yourself.

First-Time Home Buyers' Tax Credit

Up to $1,500 back at tax time

A $10,000 non-refundable tax credit that puts up to $1,500 directly back in your pocket when you file your taxes the year you buy.

Land Transfer Tax Rebate

Up to $4,000 rebate in Ontario

Ontario offers first-time buyers a rebate on land transfer tax—up to $4,000 that you don't have to pay at closing.

Cash-Back Incentives

Lender programs for closing costs

Certain lenders offer cash-back programs that can help cover your closing costs or fund immediate renovations. I know which ones offer the best deals.

New to Canada Program

Options for newcomers

Moved to Canada recently? There are mortgage programs specifically designed for newcomers—even without established Canadian credit history. I'll help you find the right fit.

Before You Start

What you need to know

1

Your credit score matters—but it's not everything

Yes, your credit score affects your rate. But if yours isn't perfect, don't panic. I'll walk you through exactly what lenders are looking for and give you practical tips to improve your score before you apply.

2

The stress test is real

Every buyer has to qualify at a higher rate than they'll actually pay. I'll explain how this works and what it means for your budget so there are no surprises when we run the numbers.

3

Mortgage insurance vs. life insurance

Your lender will offer you mortgage insurance at closing. It's not the same as life insurance, and it's not always the best option. I'll explain the difference so you can make an informed decision.

4

Pre-approval gives you power

Walking into a showing with a pre-approval letter tells sellers you're serious. It also locks in your rate for up to 120 days, protecting you if rates go up while you're shopping.
