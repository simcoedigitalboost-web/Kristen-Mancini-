New Construction

Building new or buying pre-construction?

New builds have their own financing requirements—progress draws, completion mortgages, and bridge loans. I'll coordinate the moving pieces so you can focus on watching your home come together.

Two common scenarios

Whether you're building a custom home or buying a pre-construction condo, the financing works differently than a standard purchase. Here's what you need to know:

Building a Custom Home

When you're working with a builder on your own lot, you'll need a construction mortgage with progress draws. The lender releases funds in stages as construction milestones are completed—foundation, framing, lockup, and final completion. I'll coordinate with your builder and the lender to keep everything on track.

Buying Pre-Construction

Purchasing from a developer? You'll make deposits during construction and need your full mortgage ready at closing—which could be months or years away. I'll help you get pre-approved, lock in rates when the time is right, and ensure you're ready when the builder hands over the keys.

Bridge financing

Selling your current home and buying a new one, but the closing dates don't line up? A bridge loan covers the gap—giving you access to your existing equity before your sale closes so you can complete your purchase.

Bridge loans are short-term (usually 30-90 days) and based on the firm sale of your current property. I'll help you determine if bridge financing makes sense for your situation and find the best terms.

Why timing matters

Construction timelines are unpredictable. Builders run behind, permits get delayed, weather happens. Your financing needs to be flexible enough to accommodate these realities.

I work with lenders who understand construction and won't penalize you if your completion date shifts. Let's build a financing plan that works with your project, not against it.
