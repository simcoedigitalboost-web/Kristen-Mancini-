Why homeowners refinance

Refinancing means replacing your current mortgage with a new one‚Äîusually for a higher amount. The difference between what you owe and what your home is worth (your equity) gets turned into cash you can use.

Things to consider

Refinancing isn't free, and it's not always the right move. Here's what we'll talk through together to make sure it makes sense for your situation:

1

Breaking your current mortgage

If you're mid-term, there may be penalties. We'll calculate whether the savings outweigh the costs.

2

The 80% rule

You can typically borrow up to 80% of your home's value. We'll figure out how much equity you can access.

3

Qualification requirements

You'll need to requalify under current rules. Income, credit, and debt ratios all matter.

4

Long-term cost

Extending your amortization means more interest over time. We'll look at the full picture.

How much could you access?

A quick example: If your home is worth $600,000 and you owe $350,000, you have $250,000 in equity. At 80% loan-to-value, you could potentially refinance up to $480,000‚Äîgiving you access to $130,000 in cash.

Every situation is different, and actual numbers depend on current rates, your income, and lender policies. Let's run the real numbers together.
