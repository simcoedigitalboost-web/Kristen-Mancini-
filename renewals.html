Mortgage Renewals

Don't just sign what they send

Your bank is counting on you to accept their renewal offer without shopping around. That's great for them—not so great for you. I can usually find you a better deal.

The renewal trap

About 120 days before your mortgage matures, you'll get a letter from your lender with a renewal offer. Most people sign it and send it back without a second thought. After all, it's easy—no paperwork, no hassle.

But here's what they don't tell you: that offer is almost never their best rate. They're hoping you'll value convenience over savings. And over a 5-year term, even a small rate difference can cost you thousands.

of Canadians just sign

When to start thinking about renewal

The best time to start shopping your renewal is 4-6 months before your maturity date. This gives us time to explore options without rushing—and lets you lock in a rate while we shop.

Switching is easier than you think

Worried about the hassle of changing lenders? Don't be. In most cases, the new lender covers all the legal and administrative costs. You get a better rate without paying a dime for the switch.

And if staying with your current lender actually is the best option? That's fine too. At least you'll know you're getting a competitive rate—not just the first number they threw at you.
