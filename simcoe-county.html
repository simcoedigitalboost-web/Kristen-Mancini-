Serving Simcoe County

Mortgages across Simcoe County

From the shores of Georgian Bay to the farmland of Essa Township, I help families throughout Simcoe County find the right mortgage for their situation.

A county of diverse communities

Simcoe County is one of Ontario's largest and most varied regions. Beach towns, ski villages, agricultural communities, and growing suburban centers—each with its own character and real estate market.

Whether you're looking for a family home in Innisfil, a beachfront property in Wasaga, or a historic house in Penetanguishene, I can help you navigate the local market and find financing that works.

Wasaga Beach

The world's longest freshwater beach. Popular with retirees, vacationers, and increasingly, young families seeking affordability.

Collingwood

Four-season resort town at the base of Blue Mountain. Strong demand from skiers, outdoor enthusiasts, and Toronto weekenders.

Innisfil

One of the GTA's fastest-growing communities. GO Train access and new developments draw young families from the city.

Midland

Historic waterfront town on Georgian Bay. Healthcare hub for the region with growing demand for housing.

Penetanguishene

Bilingual community with deep history and beautiful waterfront. Quieter alternative to busier lake towns.

Bradford West Gwillimbury

Agricultural roots meeting suburban growth. Major expansion planned with new GO station coming.

Every corner of the county

I work with clients throughout Simcoe County—not just the major towns. If you're buying in one of our smaller communities, I understand the unique considerations that come with rural properties, well and septic systems, and properties with acreage.

Rural and waterfront expertise

Many Simcoe County properties don't fit the standard suburban mold. Hobby farms, waterfront lots, properties with seasonal access—these all have unique financing considerations.

I work with lenders who understand rural properties and can accommodate things like well water, septic systems, and larger acreages. If your bank says no, there's often still a path forward.
